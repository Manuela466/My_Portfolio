Projects

Gene Expression Analysis of MMP2 and MMP9 in Response to Scaffold Treatment

This project analyzed RNA-seq data to investigate how ion-modified calcium phosphate scaffolds affect MMP2 and MMP9 expression in mouse macrophages. Using FastQC, MultiQC, HISAT2, SAMtools, StringTie, and R, we processed, quantified, and visualized gene expression across three scaffold treatments. MMP9 expression was highest in the zinc-based scaffold group, while MMP2 was not detectably expressed, suggesting treatment-specific differences in macrophage tissue-remodeling and inflammatory responses.

The role of Gut Microbiome in Anorexia Nervosa

This project examines how alterations in the gut microbiome influence brain pathways associated with appetite and behavior. Using 16S rRNA sequencing data from an activity-based anorexia (ABA) mouse model, We processed and analyzed microbial diversity using a reproducible DADA2 pipeline. These results lay the foundation for understanding microbiome-driven influences on hypothalamic gene expression.

Bulk RNA-seq Analysis of Type 2 Diabetic vs Control Skin

This project analyzed bulk RNA-seq data from Type 2 diabetic and non-diabetic skin tissue to identify changes in gene expression. We used FastQC, MultiQC, STAR, featureCounts, and DESeq2 to process the data and evaluate differentially expressed genes. The analysis revealed disruptions in cell adhesion, tissue structure, lipid metabolism, and wound-healing pathways, providing insight into the molecular basis of diabetic skin complications.
